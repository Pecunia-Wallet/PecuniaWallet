Can Pecunia Wallet freeze a user’s wallet?

No. Pecunia Wallet cannot freeze or take possession of virtual currency on law enforcement’s behalf or send virtual currency from the user’s wallet to another party. The private keys for the virtual currency are held by the user and encrypted locally on the user’s device.

Why does Pecunia Wallet, because of its non-custodial software, generally not have identifying information about its users?

The Pecunia Wallet Platform provides an easy-to-use software interface that permits users to manage virtual currency on their desktop computers and mobile phones. The software provided by Pecunia Wallet permits users of the software to hold, send and receive virtual currency, with each user having total independent control over the private keys of their virtual currency. The private keys for the virtual currency are held by the user and are encrypted locally on the user’s device. Pecunia Wallet does not have accounts, custody or control over a user’s virtual currency. Consequently, unless a user contacts Pecunia Wallet and provides it with such information, Pecunia Wallet does not have any information about a user’s name, email address, address, or national ID number.
